## Writing principles

**Lead with the problem, then the relief**
Start with what hurts in real life, then show how it gets better.

**Be concrete**
Use examples, outcomes, and situations people recognize.

**Respect skepticism**
Assume the reader has seen hype before. Don't oversell. Explain what's real.

**Avoid "feature soup"**
Talk in workflows and outcomes, not a list of capabilities.

**Be short on adjectives, strong on verbs**
Every sentence should move things forward. Cut filler.

## Self-check

Before publishing, ask:
1. Would I say this to a smart friend over coffee?
2. Is there a specific outcome or example?
3. Could a competitor say the same thing? (If yes, it's too generic.)
4. Am I leading with the problem or the product?
5. Would a skeptic roll their eyes? (If yes, dial it back.)
