# Sales Playbook

How we think about selling.

---

## Philosophy

We believe in understanding the customer's world before talking about ours. Discovery first, demo second. If we can't articulate their problem better than they can, we haven't earned the right to pitch.

**Core beliefs:**
- The best sales conversations feel like consulting, not pitching
- Customers buy when they feel understood, not when they feel impressed
- Specifics beat superlatives — "saves 3 hours per week" beats "revolutionary"
- If the product isn't right for them, say so early. Trust compounds.

---

## Approach

### Before the call

- Read everything we have on the account (`accounts/[name]/`)
- Check their website, LinkedIn, recent news
- Have a hypothesis about their problem — but hold it loosely
- Prepare 3-5 discovery questions, not a slide deck

### During the call

**Discovery phase (60-70% of the time):**
- Start with their world, not yours
- Ask open-ended questions about how they work today
- Listen for pain language — write it down in their words
- Understand who else is involved in the decision

**Positioning phase (20-30%):**
- Connect their specific pain to what you do
- Use their language, not your marketing language
- Show, don't tell — demo the workflow that addresses their pain
- Be honest about what's live vs. coming

**Next steps (5-10%):**
- Always end with a clear next step
- Propose a specific action, not "let's stay in touch"

### After the call

- Update the account in `accounts/[name]/`
- Log follow-ups as action items
- Send a follow-up within 24 hours

---

## What we don't do

- Cold pitch without research
- Lead with a demo before understanding the problem
- Promise timelines we can't keep
- Discount to close — if price is the only objection, it's not the right fit
- Use pressure tactics ("limited time offer", "other companies are signing up")

---

## Qualification

### Good signals
- They describe a real pain, not a theoretical interest
- They've tried to solve it before (shows urgency)
- The person on the call can influence the decision
- They're asking detailed questions about how it works

### Red flags
- "Just exploring" with no specific pain
- No budget authority and no path to it
- They want a feature you don't have and won't build
- The timeline is "someday"
