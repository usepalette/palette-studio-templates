# Content Playbook

How we think about content.

---

## Philosophy

Content should be useful first, promotional second. If someone reads a piece and learns something — even if they never buy — we've done our job. The best content comes from real work: problems we've solved, decisions we've made, things we've learned.

**Core beliefs:**
- Write from experience, not research alone
- One strong idea beats five mediocre ones
- Distribution matters as much as creation — a great post nobody sees is a wasted post
- Consistency beats intensity — regular output over sporadic bursts
- Every piece should have one clear takeaway

---

## Approach

### Before writing

- Check `foundations/brand/voice.md` — know how we sound
- Check `work/content/` — has this been covered before?
- Define the one thing the reader should take away
- Know where this will be published (LinkedIn, blog, email) — format accordingly

### While writing

- Lead with the insight or problem, not the backstory
- Use concrete examples — specific beats abstract
- Keep paragraphs short. One idea per paragraph.
- Read it out loud. If you stumble, rewrite.
- Cut 20% after the first draft. Then cut 10% more.

### After writing

- Does it pass the voice self-check in `foundations/brand/voice.md`?
- Would a skeptic find it credible?
- Is the takeaway clear in the first 3 sentences?

## Content types

| Type | Length | Where | Cadence |
|------|--------|-------|---------|
| LinkedIn post | 100-200 words | LinkedIn | 2-3x/week |
| Blog post | 500-1500 words | Blog | 1-2x/month |
| Case study | 800-1200 words | Website | As earned |
| Email newsletter | 300-500 words | Email | Weekly/biweekly |

---

## Distribution

Publishing is half the job. For every piece:

1. **Primary channel** — where it lives (blog, LinkedIn, etc.)
2. **Amplification** — share in relevant communities, Slack groups, threads
3. **Repurpose** — can a blog post become 3 LinkedIn posts? Can a case study become a talk?

---

## What we don't do

- Write generic "thought leadership" with no original insight
- Publish AI-generated content without heavy editing and a real human perspective
- Chase SEO keywords without having something genuine to say
- Write about topics we have no direct experience with
